# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Jobdun serves builders, trades, crews, and platform admins in the Australian construction and trades market. Builders post work and manage applicants. Trades and crews browse jobs, apply, message, and upload verification documents. Admins review platform activity, moderate users and jobs, handle verification queues, broadcast updates, and inspect audit trails.

These users are task-focused. They are usually trying to move work forward, verify trust, resolve risk, or make a decision quickly rather than explore a soft consumer experience.

## Product Purpose

Jobdun connects construction businesses with verified trades and crews. The mobile app supports marketplace workflows for builders and trades. The separate admin web app gives authorized Jobdun staff operational control over moderation, verifications, users, jobs, broadcasts, payments, and audit visibility.

Success means users can find work, post work, prove credentials, and resolve admin workflows with confidence. Admin success means high-risk items are visible, status is unambiguous, and actions are traceable.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, practical, industrial.

The product should feel built for people who work with their hands: dense, direct, confident, and useful. Copy should be declarative and specific. The interface should never feel apologetic, playful, decorative, or like a generic SaaS pitch deck.

## Anti-references

Avoid consumer lifestyle app softness, generic SaaS dashboards, white marketing surfaces, ghost buttons, gradient decoration, thin typography, ornamental cards, friendly illustrations, and over-explained microcopy.

Avoid confusing this product with the unrelated New Zealand JobDUN internship site. This Jobdun is an Australian construction and trades workforce platform.

## Design Principles

1. Put the job to be done first. Every screen should make the next operational decision obvious.
2. Earn trust through visible status, auditability, and clear consequences.
3. Use density deliberately. Admins need scannable data, not decorative space.
4. Prefer direct language. Buttons, empty states, and errors should say what happened and what action comes next.
5. Preserve the industrial identity. The interface should feel heavy, grounded, and operational.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA is required. Text contrast, UI component contrast, touch target floors, reduced motion handling, and semantic state indicators are non-negotiable. Color must never be the only way to communicate status. The app must support practical use by people in field and admin contexts, including constrained attention, variable lighting, keyboard navigation on web, and OS text-size needs.
