# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Jobdun serves Australian construction and trades users in two primary roles:
builders who post jobs and manage applicants, and trades workers or crews who
browse jobs, apply, upload verification documents, and track application status.
Users are often task-focused, mobile-first, and need fast decisions under real
worksite conditions rather than a decorative or marketing-heavy experience.

## Product Purpose

Jobdun is a workforce marketplace for matching construction jobs with verified
trades. The mobile app helps builders publish work, review applications, message
applicants, and make hiring decisions. It helps trades track opportunities,
submit quotes, prove credentials, and understand where each application stands.
Success means the UI makes job status, trust, and the next action obvious.

## Brand Personality

Aggressive, practical, and confident. The interface should feel built for people
who work with their hands: heavy visual weight, direct labels, dense task-focused
layouts, and no apologetic or playful tone.

## Anti-references

Do not make Jobdun look like a generic SaaS dashboard, soft consumer lifestyle
app, or VC pitch deck. Avoid white backgrounds, ghost buttons, decorative
gradients, frosted glass, thin typography, over-friendly copy, decorative motion,
and hidden controls that make users hunt for core actions.

## Design Principles

1. Put status first: every job and application surface should make state,
   ownership, and next action visible without exploration.
2. Use weight with purpose: bold type, strong borders, filled actions, and dense
   spacing should support fast scanning, not decoration.
3. Keep trust legible: verification, identity, pricing, and status must be
   encoded with text and iconography, never color alone.
4. Preserve task flow: prefer familiar product patterns, predictable controls,
   and low-latency interactions over expressive novelty.
5. Respect worksite context: content must remain readable, tappable, and useful
   on mobile screens with dynamic text and reduced-motion settings.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA is required. Text contrast must meet AA, interactive targets must be
at least 48dp, status cannot rely on color alone, dynamic type must be honored
within the app's clamped scaler, and motion must respect reduced-motion settings.
