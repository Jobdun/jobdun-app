Typography Audit · Branding Update

Type is a trust decision,
not a cosmetic one.

People decide whether an app or a brand feels legitimate within seconds — before they read a word — and a big part of that judgment is the type. The easier something is to read, the more the brain treats it as true, competent, and worth acting on (the "processing-fluency" effect). For a platform whose entire pitch is "verified, no timewasters," the typeface is doing trust-work on every screen.

01

What type does to a brand

8 factors that decide it
A · FLUENCY

Processing fluency

Easy-to-read type is processed faster, and the brain mislabels "easy" as "trustworthy and true." Legible body text literally makes people believe and use the product more.

Win with: a screen-tuned body face at the 16px floor.
B · TONE

Personality & voice

Type is a voice before words are read. Grotesque/geometric = built, precise, modern; condensed = urban/sporty; slab = heavy/heritage; rounded = friendly.

Match: "licensed tradies, gets it done."
C · TRUST

Credibility

Clean, consistent, well-spaced type signals competence and care. Messy or generic type reads as amateur — fatal for a marketplace selling trust.

Win with: structure + consistency.
D · HIERARCHY

Scannability

Clear display↔body contrast guides the eye, lowers cognitive load, and speeds the decision that matters (apply, post a job).

Win with: strong size/weight steps.
E · PAIRING

Contrast & harmony

Two faces need enough difference to separate roles, but enough harmony to feel intentional. Too similar = muddy; too different = chaotic.

Win with: one display + one body, distinct weights.
F · STRENGTH

Weight & structure

Heavier, structured letterforms read as strong, reliable, "built to last" — exactly what construction buyers respond to.

Win with: a heavy display weight (800–900).
G · CONSISTENCY

App ↔ marketing

One type system across the website and the app builds recognition and compounds trust. A mismatch fractures the brand.

Win with: the same two families everywhere.
H · MECHANICS

Readability settings

Size, line-height, tracking and line length affect readability as much as the font itself. Tokens must encode them, not leave them to chance.

Win with: 16px / 1.5 lh / 45–75ch.

The research-backed pick

Archivo + Inter

Strength + structure + logo-match. A normal-width grotesque with heavy weights (Black 900 echoes the trademark wordmark). Reads as built and reliable, stays modern, and is more readable than condensed type on real headlines.
BodyInter · the 2026 UI fluency leader
Every trade is licence-checked and every builder carries a verified ABN. Tall x-height and open apertures make Inter exceptionally legible at small sizes — which is exactly what UI demands.
Caption · metadata · 12px
Maximum fluency = maximum trust. Screen-tuned letterforms reduce reading effort, which the research ties directly to trust and repeat usage.
Inter body on a light marketing surface — legibility holds both themes
Builders post a job, see only licence-checked trades, and talk to them direct. Inter keeps long-form marketing copy effortless to read at 14–16px on white as well as on the dark app surface — one body face, both worlds.
Honest trade-off: Archivo and Inter are both grotesques, so the pairing has slightly less intrinsic contrast than Oswald-condensed + Open Sans. Weight (Archivo 800/900 vs Inter 400), size, and tracking carry the hierarchy instead — the net is cleaner, more trustworthy, and finally consistent with the logo.

05

Readability mechanics

the non-font half of the decision
16px

Body floor

Never below 16px for primary body on mobile. It's Google's floor and the fluency baseline.

1.5

Line-height (body)

1.5 for body, 1.1–1.2 for display. Open leading reduces reading effort and tracking errors.

45–75

Measure (line length)

Cap reading width at 45–75 characters. Long lines lose the reader between rows.

±tr

Tracking

Tighten large display (≈ −0.5px), leave body at default, add tracking to ALL-CAPS labels (+0.8–1.2px).
