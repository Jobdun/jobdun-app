Research lens: NN/g — a mobile homepage is a springboard, not a dashboard-dump; time-to-first-job is the metric that matters on a marketplace; first screenful carries ~80% of attention (F-pattern); one owner per piece of state. Current pain (from the live audit): banner + availability bar + 3 stat tiles push the first job below the fold on small phones.

Verdict

#1 Action Deck is the recommendation: it keeps the status pill + dock you just shipped, fixes the audited below-the-fold problem by compressing stats to a micro-strip, and leads with the one thing a tradie opens the app for — "where do I stand right now?" — before the feed. #2 Feed-First is the budget path (smallest delta, proven pattern) if you want jobs-at-pixel-one this week. #3 and #5 are the right *second* moves once geo-matching and aggregation data exist; #4 fits a Stage-2 "run your business" pivot. Recommended build order: ship #1, fold in #5's brief line-items as the data matures. Builder: #1 Builder Action Deck now (reuses the live tradie skeleton), evolve its hero into #2’s Applicant Inbox as volume grows; #5 Project Command is Stage 2.
