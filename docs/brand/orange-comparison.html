Safety orange: A vs B

Two oranges are in play — the registered trademark/logo orange and the current UI CTA token. They look nearly identical (contrast between them is just 1.14:1), but they behave very differently against text. Ratios below are WCAG 2.2; AA needs 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large/bold text and UI.

Verdict: adopt A · #FA5B0D

The two are visually the same orange (1.14:1 apart), so switching the UI token to the trademark colour costs you nothing in look — but it fixes the two pairs that fail today and aligns the whole site with the registered mark.

  • White-on-orange: 2.80 (fail) → 3.20 — now clears the 3:1 floor for large/bold text & UI.
  • Orange ink on light backgrounds: 2.68 (fail) → 3.06 — usable for large headings/icons in light mode without a darker fallback.
  • Dark-slate-on-orange: 6.37 → 5.58 — drops slightly but stays comfortably AA. This is Jobdun's actual button-text pairing (onAction), so CTAs stay AA either way.
  • Brand truth: #FA5B0D is the colour on the official trademark — one orange everywhere beats two near-identical ones sitting side-by-side in the nav.

One caveat: white on orange still isn't 4.5:1, so keep white labels large/bold — or keep using dark-slate text on orange CTAs (which Jobdun already does, at AA). If you switch the token, the contrast-guard test expectations get updated and re-run.

The lettering

What font the wordmark is

The JOBDUN wordmark in the trademark is drawn as vector outlines, not live text — there's no embedded font name. The style is a heavy / black-weight geometric sans-serif: wide, near-square proportions, full round counters, even stroke weight, blunt terminals.

It is not the site's display font — Jobdun headings use Oswald, which is condensed (narrow). The logo is much wider and heavier. Closest live matches if you want text to echo the mark: Archivo Black (closest), Montserrat Black/ExtraBold, or Poppins Black.
